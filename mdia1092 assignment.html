Census 2021 — Homelessness Re-rendered
By: Karen Mneimne
Published 3 Aug 2024, 7:46pm














If you’ve lived in Australia for a couple of years, then you’ve probably become
familiar with the Australian Census by the Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS).














Whether by your own free will and the internet, various governmental ad campaigns,
or being legally mandated to fill out a census questionnaire, you’ve come across it.














Now what?














Say you want to find out the results, the data the Census has come out with about the
state of the country. What’s changed since the previous census was held 5 years ago?

A quick Google search will lead you to the ABS website, where you’re swiftly bombarded
with walls of text and numbers.














You return to the search tab, scrolling down some more and come across the ABC’s
article “Census 2021 — Australia as 100 people”. A conveniently easily digestible
re-rendering of census data which delivers the results to you in bite-size chunks.














They simplified the census by representing the population of Australia “as 100 people”.
The corporation displayed this simplified data as a map of dots separated into categories where


1 dot = 1%














However, the ABC failed to include many marginalized groups, including an often
overlooked concern of the census, one that the ABS themselves took note
of and went out of their way to strategize including;








The significance of the homeless population.











Perhaps this exclusion is because this marginalized group, amongst others, cannot
be represented by 1 dot, thus showing a major flaw in the ABC’s visualization
strategy.They don’t make- up 1% of the population, but rather approximately


half a dot = 0.5%














However, homelessness ought to be counted, the nuances of this issue must not remain invisible…
So, let us look closer into this half dot statistic...








Of those experiencing homelessness in 2021:
-> 55.9% were male, an increase of 1.6% from 2016
-> 44.1% were female, an increase of 10.1% from 2016.




Young people have the highest rates of homelessness:
-> Nearly a quarter (23.0%) of all people experiencing homelessness were aged from 12 to 24 years (in green)
-> 14.4% (in yellow) were aged under 12 years




However, even these numbers are somewhat arbitrary, because when you really think about it...



what really defines homelessness?
